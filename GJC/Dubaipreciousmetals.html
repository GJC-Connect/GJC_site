DMCC Wraps Up DPMC 2025 With Record Attendance and Launches DMCC FinX

DMCC concluded the thirteenth edition of the Dubai Precious Metals Conference with record participation and the official launch of DMCC FinX, a new ecosystem designed to connect institutional capital, trade finance, global trading houses and fintech innovators. The event, held at Atlantis The Palm, brought together more than one thousand senior leaders from the global precious metals, finance, policy and technology sectors.

Under the theme "The Future of Precious Metals Tariffs, Tokenisation and Trade Flows," the conference examined geopolitical shifts, advancements in technology and new trade routes that are reshaping the global commodities landscape. Discussions highlighted the growing convergence of precious metals, finance and digital assets, including the rise of tokenisation and the expanding role of Dubai as a centre of transparency and innovation.

Keynote speakers included His Excellency Dr Thani bin Ahmed Al Zeyoudi, UAE Minister of State for Foreign Trade, Ruth Crowell, Chief Executive of the London Bullion Market Association, and global financial commentator Grant Williams. Delegates also heard from senior representatives of major global organisations such as the World Gold Council, the Shanghai Gold Exchange, CME Group and the UAE Ministry of Economy. Panel discussions covered responsible sourcing, the harmonisation of global standards and the evolving role of bullion banks in a changing economic environment.

A major highlight of the event was the formal launch of DMCC FinX. Built on DMCC's existing infrastructure including the Dubai Gold and Commodities Exchange, the Dubai Commodities Clearing Corporation, the DMCC Crypto Centre, the DMCC Tradeflow platform and partnerships with regulatory bodies such as the Dubai Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority, the new ecosystem aims to bridge real world commerce, capital markets and digital assets.

His Excellency Dr Thani bin Ahmed Al Zeyoudi emphasised the UAE's growing importance in global precious metals trade, noting that gold flows through the country reached an estimated one hundred and eighty-six billion US dollars in twenty twenty four. He highlighted how technology, tokenisation and blockchain are transforming supply chains and creating new opportunities for liquidity and efficiency.

Ahmed Bin Sulayem, Executive Chairman and Chief Executive Officer of DMCC, reiterated Dubai's position as a leading marketplace for precious metals, supported by decades of investment in infrastructure, transparency and innovation. He noted that DMCC FinX represents the next phase in strengthening the connection between finance, technology and global trade.

Building on a strong year for the UAE's precious metals sector, the conference underlined Dubai's role as a major global hub for both physical and digital commodities. With more than fifteen hundred companies active in the gold and precious metals sector within DMCC, the organisation continues to support global supply chains with world class infrastructure and connectivity.

DPMC has become one of the most influential events on the global precious metals calendar, fostering collaboration at a time of significant transformation across global markets. As trade dynamics evolve, DMCC remains committed to strengthening Dubai's role as the meeting point between East and West, and as a centre for the future of trade, technology and finance.

Sponsors for DPMC 2025 included Sam Precious Metals, HMB Gold, World Gold Council, Ahlatci Metal Refinery, Emirates Minting Factory LLC, Suntech Business Solutions DPMS, AKW Consultants and Ashoka Global, reflecting the shared commitment of the industry to innovation and responsible growth.
